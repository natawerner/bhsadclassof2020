SexEd — Regina Park

The project’s exploration of human sexuality is aimed to show its true reflection. Every story in the book shapes an understanding of what it means to be sexual, as well as it proves that our sexuality plays an important role in relation to ourselves and others. By sharing something so delicate and making it public, the project is designed to empower, inspire and liberate people in the context of sexual behaviour and perception. ‘SexEd’ gathered the stories of nearly a hundred people of different group age, sex and nationalities, who anonymously shared their fears, preferences, fantasies, insecurities, difficulties and fetishes. Some of them are quite unique, however the majority of issues are of similar origin, which once again proves that we all are living creatures with the same needs, desires and issues, which we shouldn’t be ashamed or afraid of and should be able to speak of them openly with the society, the loved ones, and, most importantly, ourselves.
