Hidden Rivers — Mikhail Selitsky

Hidden Rivers is a project that takes its focus on the three underground rivers of Moscow based on the psychogeographic idea. The objective of the project is to immerse into the atmosphere of the underground worlds. The viewer drifts in places deeply hidden from the eyes in the style of unitary urbanists of the late 50s. Immersiveness, one of the basic principles of the project. The viewer can immerse himself in an audio-visual trip by watching a short film, or get acquainted with two other parts of the project where visual and audio material is presented separately in the form of the music album and photobook. This project is very multifaceted in its message. First of all, this is an immersive journey through unknown tunnels. Second of all, the project reveals the aesthetics of such places, their atmosphere, shape and color palette. Third of all, it makes you think about the influence of the urbanized territory on people.
